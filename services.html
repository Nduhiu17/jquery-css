Stokisha is an innovative product tailored specifically to offer financial solutions to SMEs with a focus on enabling women in business, youth and people living with disabilities to access finance for business sustainability and growth.

Target Marketgrowth

Stokisha, as a product has been designed to drive financial empowerment specifically towards women entrepreneurs, youth (individuals aged 18 – 35) and persons living with disabilities who run small businesses.

The Youth

The youth are an integral part of our mission and vision. This is largely attributed to the pivotal role they play in future economic development.

The proportion of Kenya’s youth to the population is among the highest globally, presenting the economy with a vibrant manpower if put to productive use.

Target Sectors

Agribusiness

Agribusiness is the backbone of the Kenyan economy, the sector alone employs 62% of the country’s population.

Despite this, there is still a persistent absence of accurate and cost-effective methods for assessing small-scale agricultural lending risk associated with small holder farmers responsible for 70% of Kenya’s Agricultural production. The smaller agribusiness SMEs are unable to access financing due to four factors;

  • Lack of credible and verifiable data to support Lending
  • Lack of Collateral.
  • Risk Consideration due to inherent agricultural risk.

    • At Stokisha it is our aim to challenge this status quo.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing is a key driver of the government’s Big four agenda and receives only 10% of financing from Kenya’s banks.

Despite the potential identified in this sector, there is still a huge gap in terms of financing. This can be attributed to:

  • Mismatch between financing needs and what banks are willing to offer. - Working capital cycle for manufacturing is longer than 6 months.
  • Perceived risk arising from a few bad lending decisions.
  • High interest rates.

Trade

The Wholesale and Retail Sector rounds up the top 4 contributors to Kenya’s GDP.

According to a recent report on this segment, Kenya’s retail spending hit an all time high of Kes 1.8 trillion. A few factors hold true with regards to the wholesale and retail segment and SMEs.

  • Kenya is a credit market and over 80% of retail trade is credit oriented.
  • The bigger opportunity is in General and Informal retail sector - shops & kiosks.
  • Know your customer (KYC) is an impediment although licensing requirements have seen significant rise in registration.
  • Last mile credit facilities availed by the general trade in some instances.
